THE DISPLACEMENT EFFECT

IMPACT OF ONLINE CAB AGGREGATORS ON TRADITIONAL DRIVERS IN BHOPAL

Course: Business Communication

Team: Aryan, Rahul, Tanya, Vishwajeet

Date: December 2025

01. CONTEXT

TECHNOLOGICAL DISPLACEMENT

The shift Intermediate Public Transport (IPT) in Bhopal has moved from traditional "Kaali-Peeli" autos to algorithmic aggregators (Ola, Uber, Rapido).

  • The Phenomenon: "Uberisation" - Algorithmic management replacing human autonomy.
  • The Conflict: Tech giants weaponizing venture capital against unorganized labor.
  • The Lie: "Convenience" for the consumer is purchased with the "Precarity" of the driver.

02. FRAMEWORK

AIM & OBJECTIVES

PRIMARY AIM

To assess the socio-economic impact of online cab aggregators on traditional auto and taxi drivers in Bhopal.

THEORETICAL LENS

Schumpeter’s "Creative Destruction":
Innovation that incessantly revolutionizes the economic structure from within, destroying the old one.

SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES:

  1. Analyze shifts in monthly income and customer volume.
  2. Identify adaptation strategies (price wars, zone shifting).
  3. Propose policy frameworks for "Just Transition".

03. METHODOLOGY

RESEARCH DESIGN

DESIGN

Descriptive Quantitative Research

SAMPLE

N = 40 Drivers
(Stratified Convenience Sampling)

ZONES

Rani Kamlapati, MP Nagar, New Market, BHEL, Arera Colony

TIMING

Off-peak hours (10 AM - 2 PM)

06. CHALLENGES

SYSTEMIC EXCLUSION

THE DIGITAL DIVIDE

Older drivers (45+) are systematically excluded. They lack the digital literacy to navigate complex UIs, effectively making the technology a barrier to entry rather than a tool.

SEASONAL TYRANNY

During Monsoons, apps use "Surge Pricing" which benefits the platform. Traditional autos often refuse rides due to waterlogging risks, further pushing customers to apps.

85%

NEGATIVE SENTIMENT

Share of drivers viewing Aggregators as a threat

07. POLICY

THE 4-PILLAR STRATEGY

1

BHOPAL YATRA APP

Develop a government-backed, Open Network (ONDC) compatible app with Zero Commission. Model: Namma Yatri (Bangalore).

2

FARE RATIONALIZATION

Link minimum fares dynamically to fuel prices (currently ₹106/L). Stop the "Race to the Bottom".

3

DIGITAL SAATHI

Targeted training programs for the 45+ demographic to bridge the digital divide.

4

SOCIAL SECURITY

Mandatory Welfare Board registration for all gig/transport workers (Pension/Insurance).

08. CONCLUSION

A CALL FOR JUST TRANSITION

Innovation without inclusion is merely extraction.

We found clear evidence of Technological Displacement. Without immediate intervention by Q1 2026, 40,000 drivers face a choice between the informal gig economy or debt spirals.

The Question: Will we preserve the "Convenience" of the few at the cost of the livelihood of the many?
