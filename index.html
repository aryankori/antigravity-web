Introduction

Transportation is the backbone of the urban economy. For decades, "Kaali-Peeli" autos dominated Bhopal's streets.

The entry of Ola, Uber, and Rapido has disrupted this ecosystem. While consumers gain convenience, traditional drivers face an existential crisis.

"The 'Uberisation' of transport employs algorithmic management to replace human autonomy."
— Raj (2019)
Primary Field Visit: Rani Kamlapati Station

Objectives & Framework

Research Objectives

  • Assess socio-economic impact on drivers.
  • Analyze income and volume shifts.
  • Identify adaptation strategies.
  • Propose "Just Transition" policies.

Theoretical Lens

Schumpeter’s Creative Destruction

Innovation incessantly revolutionizes the economic structure from within, destroying the old one.

Destruction of Old Livelihoods

Structured Questionnaire

The Economic Erosion

Finding: A drastic shift from higher income brackets to lower ones. Many drivers earning >₹15k have fallen below ₹10k.

Impact on Daily Volume

Finding: 85% of respondents reported a "Significant Decrease" in daily ridership due to app efficiency.

Coping Mechanisms

  • Price Wars: Reducing fares to match apps (unsustainable).
  • Zone Shifting: Moving to peripheral areas like BHEL.
  • Digital Exclusion: Older drivers cannot adapt to smartphone UIs.

Recommendations: A Just Transition

1. Bhopal Yatra App

Govt-backed, Zero-commission (ONDC Model).

2. Fare Rationalization

Dynamic minimum fare linked to fuel prices (₹106/L).

3. Digital Saathi

Upskilling for drivers aged 45+.
